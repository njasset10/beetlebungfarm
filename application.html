Farm Manager


Job Description:
The Farm Manager will manage all aspects of our production and distribution, with training and support from the Farm Director. They will be participating in the foundational first year as our organization takes on the stewardship of this historic farm, which will be producing vegetables, herbs, flowers and berries on a 1 acre cultivated field along with other gardens on the 5 acre farm. As a newly developing organization, the production of consistent, quality produce using organic and sustainable methods with a focus on regenerative farming practices is a primary focus. Field tasks are mostly done by hand and will include greenhouse propagation, transplanting, seeding, weeding, irrigating, cultivating, harvesting, washing and general field maintenance. This will be a production focused position, but as our farm is in a central location of our community and has an active farm stand with many visitors, the Farm Manager will need to be flexible in scheduling daily tasks to accommodate the community and other farm programs as they develop. Additionally a freedom to bring new ideas, outside expertise and continuing education is encouraged.

Manager Responsibilities:
  • Manage all the tasks and responsibilities of a working greenhouse, farm, farm stand and weekly farmers market
  • Assist in all aspects of infrastructure, including but not limited to fencing, irrigation, etc.
  • Develop and lead a farm crew.
  • Work collaboratively with Farm Director
  • Assist in implementation of farm education, volunteers and special events as needed
  • Serve as a representative of Beetlebung Farm to the community
  • Problem solve independently and collaboratively on a daily basis in response to ongoing observation and assessment of all aspects of the farm’s operations

Skills and Experience:
  • 3-5 year experience farming on a vegetable producing farm; 2-3 years experience in a management role
  • Demonstrated knowledge of organic farming practices including crop rotation, cover cropping, composting, IPM, and soil fertility management Comfortable with small animal management such as rabbits and chickens
  • Ability to lead and manage farm crew
  • Ability to hustle and thrive under pressure
  • Physically capable of all farm labor tasks
  • Excellent work ethic; passionate about farming
  • Comfortable working collaboratively and independently
  • Friendly and engaging attitude with interest in connecting to the community
  • Valid driver's license

Schedule and Compensation:
  • Overall package and salary based upon experience
  • Comfortable year round farm manager housing on site
  • Concierge healthcare service

We have much to do in this first year and will need to hit the ground running! Our Farm Manager will have the unique opportunity to be part of a small team reimagining the design and new life of Beetlebung Farm and its evolution over the coming years.

While we envision our Farm Manager position as generally geared around a 45 - 50 hour work week during the growing season (with greatly reduced weekly hours in the winter), this inaugural season may require additional time and commitment.

Start date for our Farm Manager is early to mid-March. We will begin reviewing applications immediately. If you are interested in joining the Beetlebung Farm team, please fill out the form below or email us your resume, cover letter, and references with contact information to info@beetlebungfarm.org. Please also visit our website which is under construction to learn more about who we are: www.beetlebungfarm.org.


Farm Crew Member


Job Description:
We’re seeking a farm crew member to be an integral part of our team and to work primarily in the field. Field tasks are mostly done by hand and will include greenhouse propagation, transplanting, seeding, weeding, irrigating, cultivating, harvesting, washing and general field maintenance. We’re looking for someone who is eager to learn, a good team player and willing to work hard to get the task done and to have fun doing it. Our Farm Crew Member will be participating in the foundational first year as our organization takes on the stewardship of this historic farm, which will be producing vegetables, herbs, flowers and berries on a 1 acre cultivated field along with other gardens on the 5 acre farm. As a newly developing organization the production of consistent, quality produce using organic and sustainable methods with a focus on regenerative farming practices is a primary focus. This is a unique opportunity to be part of a small team reimagining the design and new life of this farm and its evolution. Overall, our goal is to support team members in developing and deepening their skills, with opportunities to continue to grow with the farm.

Farm Crew Member Responsibilities:
  • Harvesting
  • Post harvest handling: washing, sorting, packing, delivering
  • Fieldwork: planting, weeding, mulching, maintenance and more
  • Greenhouse work: seeding, thinning, transplanting, etc.
  • Stocking and upkeep of our farm stand
  • Help with farmers markets, and occasional on-farm events

Skills and Experience:
  • 1-3 years experience working on a producing farm
  • Ability to hustle and to thrive under pressure
  • Physically capable of all farm labor tasks (comfortably lift 50 lbs)
  • Excellent work ethic; passionate about farming
  • Comfortable working collaboratively and independently
  • Valid driver's license


Start date for our Farm Crew is early to mid-April. We will begin reviewing applications immediately.
